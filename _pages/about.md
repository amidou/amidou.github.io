I am a radio astronomer studying the **neutral atomic hydrogen (HI)** content of galaxies
and what it reveals about their evolution. My work focuses on how environment —
from isolated field galaxies to dense compact groups — shapes the cold gas reservoirs
that fuel star formation, and how **angular momentum** connects a galaxy's past to its
future structural and kinematic state.

I use the [MeerKAT telescope](https://www.sarao.ac.za/science/meerkat/) and other
radio facilities to map HI at 21 cm, tracing gas stripping, accretion, and the subtle
imprints of galaxy–galaxy interaction across a wide range of environments.
Current projects include the MeerKAT view of **Hickson Compact Groups** and the
**AMIGA sample** of isolated galaxies, which together bracket the full range of galaxy
environments and allow us to cleanly disentangle internal processes from external ones.

Before joining IAA-CSIC, I completed my PhD at the University of Cape Town under
the supervision of Prof. Claude Carignan, and held a fellowship at the
IAU Office of Astronomy for Development. I contributed to early MeerKAT science
and its pathfinder KAT-7, and remain interested in the prospects of SKA-era surveys
for resolving open questions about what governs a galaxy's HI fraction across
mass, environment, and cosmic epoch.
